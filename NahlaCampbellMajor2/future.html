Descendents Keyblade

I had this idea after being obsessed with Descendents from Disney and Square Enix's Kingdom Hearts. To be honest, Descendents is actually an awful franchise that's badly written and main character is completely unlikable. I'm only there for Uma, who is very unfairly made to be a villain considering the circumstances of the films. But I had this idea the Kingdom Hearts and Descendents were in the same universe and Descendents took place in the future after Sora's adventures. I don't have logistics for how these two universe's can come together considering the different rules the govern them, but this really gave me a good excuse to make a keyblade for the only good character, Uma.

I am extremely excited for this project! I don't know when I'll have time to do something this big and elaborate and maybe it's too soon to take on something like this, but I'm just excited.

The design is based off of Uma's outfits and aesthetics in the movies and the structure is based off the Wheel of Fate keyblade from Kingdom Hearts III. Uma's entire motif is centered around pirates and ocean and her main color is teal, so I tried to include as much of the color as I could without it being overwhelming. And, since Uma is the daughter of our poor, unfortunate Ursula, I also made her hilt in the shape of an octopus with four tentacles for the guard and another two for the grip. The remaining two legs will serve as a "keychain" of sorts that will have the symbols for her first mate, Harry son of Captain Hook, and her second mate, Gil son of Gaston.

The Wheel of Fate keyblade features shrouds on either side of the dowel. I, instead, did a shroud on one side that will have seashells attached and the flag for Uma's ship on the other side. I added a small twist with Uma's flag by adding words that I closely associate with her such as freedom, anarchy, and revenge. And, finally, while the Wheel of Fate keyblade has a helm, or ship wheel, as the actual "blade" part that would deliver the hardest hits. I replaced that part with an anchor and nautlius shell with teal and cerulean coral coming out of it.

In all truthfulness, I'm not quite sure what I want to make this entire thing out of. I defintely want to use wooden dowels to make a 'T' cross for the base, string for the shroud, and canvas fabric for the flag. Then, there's the rest of it, which I'm most nervous but excited for. I was thinking about making the hilt and blade out of clay, but with the size of it, it might be too heavy for the dowels to hold that weight and also too heavy for me to carry around at conventions. Maybe I could use foam? But foam may be a little too fragile and conventions are always bustling with movement and I'd hate for it to get knocked into and fall apart. If I use enough hot glue, then perhaps it'll stay down pretty well. As the DIY moms always say, if you burn your hands enough, then it'll surely stay in place. The painting will also be really fun. I'll need a lot of teal paint and I was thinking of adding gold accents to the nautilus as well. I was also thinking of getting some polish spray to give it a really glossy finish.

All in all, I don't when I'm going to find the time and money to get around to this project, but when it comes, it's going to be really exciting and fun. Even if I get a little frustrated in the process, that's to be expected. Not every project will go by the blueprint; sometimes, you'll come up with new ideas or have to improvise. Getting to the end goal and seeing the final result is sweet, but the process is even sweeter.
